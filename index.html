Our Timeline

September 19

I don't think I fully understood what was happening the moment I first saw you — September 19, just an ordinary day that somehow became the beginning of everything. I was just a first-year student trying to figure out where I belonged, and then there was you — a graduate student, someone who already knew so much about the world, about life. I remember thinking: why would someone like her ever notice someone like me? But you did. And that still feels like the most beautiful accident.

October 16

October 16 is the day I want to remember forever. The day I stopped wondering and started knowing — that you were someone I wanted to walk beside, for as long as you'd let me. Every day since then has felt like something I don't deserve but am endlessly grateful for. You make ordinary moments feel worth keeping. That's why I built this — because some things are too important to only live inside a memory. These photos, these words, this little corner of the internet — it's all just my way of saying: I see you. I cherish you. And I don't want to forget a single day of this.

Still writing...

We are two introverted people who somehow became each other's most comfortable place. I don't know how it happened — one day I looked up and realized I was laughing louder, talking more, being someone I didn't know I could be. She did that. Without even trying. We crossed borders for each other. Went back to Mongolia, had our dates there, came back together — like we were already practicing what a life side by side could feel like. Most nights I'm not in my dorm. I'm where she is. And that feels more like home than anywhere else. My mom still doesn't know why I won't stop smiling and going out so much, keeping me busy.
